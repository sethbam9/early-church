## New passages (12)

### Against Heresies (src-irenaeus-against-heresies)
- psg-irenaeus-ah-1-10-1 — AH 1.10.1: The Church dispersed throughout the whole world received from the apostles this faith: one God Father Almighty; one Christ Jesus Son of God who became incarnate; Holy Spirit who proclaimed through prophets the birth from a virgin, passion, resurrection from the dead, ascension; future gathering and raising of all flesh.
- psg-irenaeus-ah-1-10-2 — AH 1.10.2: The Church, though dispersed throughout Germany, Spain, Gaul, East, Egypt, Libya, preserves this faith with one soul and one heart; no ruler of the Churches teaches anything different; faith neither added to by the eloquent nor diminished by the slow.
- psg-irenaeus-ah-3-1-1 — AH 3.1.1: Matthew issued his Gospel among the Hebrews in their own dialect while Peter and Paul were preaching at Rome. After their departure Mark handed down Peter's preaching; Luke recorded Paul's; John published his Gospel at Ephesus.
- psg-irenaeus-ah-3-2-1 — AH 3.2.1-2: Heretics, when confuted from Scriptures, accuse Scriptures of being incorrect; when referred to the apostolic tradition preserved by succession of presbyters in the Churches, they object to that too — they consent neither to Scripture nor to tradition.
- psg-irenaeus-ah-3-3-1 — AH 3.3.1: It is within the power of all in every Church to contemplate the tradition of the apostles manifested throughout the whole world and to reckon up those who were instituted bishops by the apostles, and their succession to our own times.
- psg-irenaeus-ah-3-3-2 — AH 3.3.2: The tradition derived from the apostles of the great, ancient, universally known Church founded at Rome by Peter and Paul — every Church must agree with this Church on account of its preeminent authority (potiorem principalitatem); faith preached to men which comes down by means of the successions of the bishops.
- psg-irenaeus-ah-3-3-3 — AH 3.3.3: Rome bishop list: Peter+Paul → Linus → Anacletus → Clement (who knew the apostles) → Evaristus → Alexander → Sixtus → Telephorus (gloriously martyred) → Hyginus → Pius → Anicetus → Soter → Eleutherius (12th from apostles). "In this order, and by this succession, the ecclesiastical tradition from the apostles, and the preaching of the truth, have come down to us."
- psg-irenaeus-ah-3-3-4 — AH 3.3.4: Polycarp was instructed by apostles, conversed with many who had seen Christ, and was appointed bishop of Smyrna by apostles in Asia; Irenaeus saw him in early youth. Polycarp came to Rome in the time of Anicetus and caused many to turn from heretics to the Church. Polycarp called Marcion "first-born of Satan."
- psg-irenaeus-ah-3-4-1 — AH 3.4.1: The apostles lodged in the Church all things pertaining to truth — she is the entrance to life, all others are thieves and robbers; in any dispute, recourse is to the most ancient Churches with which the apostles held constant intercourse.
- psg-irenaeus-ah-3-4-3 — AH 3.4.3: Valentinus came to Rome in the time of Hyginus; Cerdon (Marcion's predecessor) arrived in the time of Hyginus; Marcion flourished under Anicetus — thus all these heretical teachers post-date the apostolic Church and cannot trace to the apostles.
- psg-irenaeus-ah-4-18-1 — AH 4.18.1-2: The oblation of the Church is accounted a pure sacrifice acceptable to God throughout all the world; the class of oblations has not been set aside — sacrifices existed among the Jews and exist now in the Church; only the species changed (from slaves to freemen offering first-fruits).
- psg-irenaeus-ah-5-2-2 — AH 5.2.2-3: The cup is the communion of His blood; the bread we break is the communion of His body. Our bodies, being nourished by the eucharist — by the body and blood of Christ — shall rise at their appointed time; the Word of God grants them resurrection to the glory of God the Father.
